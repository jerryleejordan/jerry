Cognitive Behavioural Therapy

28 Jul 2023

What is Cognitive Behavioural Therapy

Cognitive Behavioural Therapy (CBT) - also referred to as Cognitive Therapy - is a widely recognised and highly effective form of psychotherapy that focuses on identifying and modifying negative thought patterns and behaviours. It is based on the idea that our thoughts, feelings, and actions are interconnected, and by changing our thoughts and behaviours, we can positively impact our emotional well-being.

Principles of CBT

The fundamental principle of CBT is to help individuals become aware of their automatic negative thoughts, which often contribute to emotional distress and maladaptive behaviours. These thoughts are often referred to as cognitive distortions and may include all-or-nothing thinking, overgeneralisation, catastrophising, and self-blame.

CBT Process

CBT is typically structured and goal-oriented. The therapist and the individual work collaboratively to identify specific issues or problems that are causing distress. Through various techniques and exercises, the individual learns to challenge and replace these negative thought patterns with more balanced and rational thoughts.

Behavioural Change in CBT

In addition to cognitive restructuring, CBT also incorporates behavioural interventions. By modifying behaviours that contribute to distress, individuals can experience a positive shift in their emotions. behavioural techniques may include exposure therapy to confront fears, behavioural experiments to test negative beliefs, and skill-building exercises to enhance coping abilities.

Effectiveness of CBT

CBT has been extensively researched and has demonstrated effectiveness in treating a wide range of mental health conditions, including anxiety disorders, depression, phobias, Post-Traumatic Stress Disorder (PTSD), and Obsessive-Compulsive Disorder (OCD). It is often used in conjunction with other therapeutic approaches or as part of a comprehensive treatment plan.

CBT tools and coping strategies

One of the strengths of CBT is that it equips individuals with practical tools and coping strategies that can be applied in their daily lives. By empowering individuals to take an active role in their therapy and providing them with lasting skills, CBT facilitates long-term positive change and emotional resilience.
